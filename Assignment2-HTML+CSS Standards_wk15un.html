What Barack Obama said about Sidney Crosby at the White House after Winning the Staney Cup according to NHL.com

In 2009, when President Barack Obama welcomed the Stanley Cup champion Pittsburgh Penguins to the White House, he made it a point to congratulate the youngest captain ever to win the Cup, 21-year-old Sidney Crosby. Toward the end of the ceremony, Obama got a big laugh when he threw a little shade Crosby's way, remarking, "Sidney must be really fast because there's some big hockey players -- and he's not one of them."

How Sidney Crosby does not ease up during practice according to NHL.com

"Fortunately, the president got the chance to make amends. In October 2016, the champion Penguins returned to the White House, and Obama said: "Last time he was here, I took a cheap shot at Sid for his size. I wasn't the first one, on or off the ice. Of course since then, he's won two Olympic golds (2010, 2014), a World Championship (2015), a World Cup MVP (2016), a playoff MVP (2016), another NHL MVP (2014), a mountain of other postseason awards. ... And, of course, he hoisted this trophy right here, his second Stanley Cup.".
